01 — The setup

The briefing changed the question. Not whether to start, but where to start.

When we last spoke, the conversation was about depth: the brand architecture, the regional map, the distributor reality. That depth is exactly why this proposal looks the way it does. Rather than spread thin across three brand pages, five regions, and every channel at once, the scope concentrates on the two surfaces that create the pull and the two motions that turn that pull into pipeline.

Geneo and TriPollar are the demand brands. Geneo carries the science-led treatment story, TriPollar carries the home-device audience. Meta and LinkedIn are where those audiences and the distributors who serve them already are. A steady, disciplined organic rhythm on those two surfaces is the foundation everything else builds on.

On top of that foundation sit two compounding layers. A demand-generation layer of landing pages, emails, and campaign management that captures attention and routes it. And an SEO layer that quietly builds the searchable authority a science-led brand should own. One integrated retainer. One pod. Nine months to prove the system before scaling it.

02 — What we heard

Six things from the briefing that shaped this scope.

  • /01

    Geneo carries the pull. Build there first.

    Around 80 percent of current investment sits behind Geneo, and it earned that focus. Concentrating the organic engine on Geneo and TriPollar puts the work where demand is actually created, instead of diluting it across a corporate page that can wait for the architecture conversation.

  • /02

    The science is the under-told story.

    Pollogen has the clinical credibility most brands would invest heavily to claim, and today the communication leans toward beauty and lifestyle while the science backbone stays quiet. That white space anchors both the organic pillars and the blog content in the SEO scope.

  • /03

    Distributors are the first audience we win.

    Pollogen sells through distributors, not direct to clinics. Content has to give reps shareable proof and a stronger reason to order more. Demand-gen landing pages and emails are built as enablement assets, not just consumer funnels, so the distributor layer is served on every motion.

  • /04

    Tight budgets sharpen the work. They do not shrink it.

    Paid is a distribution layer here, not a demand-gen engine. We manage the campaigns, the targeting, and the optimization. The media spend itself stays client-side. The discipline of a focused budget makes the targeting better, not the ambition smaller.

  • /05

    An in-house esthetician removes the production bottleneck.

    The practicing esthetician at the Israel office is available for treatments, photo, and video on demand. We design the content systems to fully use that loop, which is how a reduced-volume organic scope still produces credible, treatment-led content every week.

  • /06

    Depth before tactics, then prove it before scaling.

    The right move is a focused system that holds for two to three quarters, generates real baseline data, and earns the case to expand. This nine-month scope is built to compound, with every other surface and region one scope amendment away.

04 — Who we're talking to

Three audiences. One direction of travel.

Every piece of content in this system is built for a specific audience at a specific moment. Distributors are first. Aestheticians follow. End consumers complete the loop. That order is not accidental. It is the reason demand travels.

Priority 01

Distributors

The first audience we win

Pollogen sells through distributors, not directly to clinics. Content cannot just create visibility. It has to give reps shareable proof points, equip them for clinic conversations, and make them confident enough to order more. When the distributor wins, everything downstream follows.

05 — The 6-month roadmap

From signature to a system that compounds.

The onboarding path is the same regardless of how the system scales later. Volume and surface area follow this scope; the rigour behind each phase does not. The first six months build the engine, gather the baseline, and earn the case for what comes next across the full nine-month term.

Pre-launch
Weeks 1–2 · Foundation

Foundation

  • Account brief: brand architecture for Geneo and TriPollar, audience hierarchy, tone per surface
  • Channel audit across Meta and LinkedIn, both pages: gaps, overlap, follower benchmarks
  • Competitive teardown: HydraFacial, Medicube, YA-MAN per platform
  • On-page SEO audit kicks off; keyword and topic map drafted
  • Month 1 editorial calendar drafted, four weeks ahead from day one
Month 1
Go live · Publishing begins

Go live

  • Both pages optimized and live on Meta and LinkedIn
  • Science series launches: OxyGeneo and Bohr Effect explainers
  • Before / after curation begins from the in-house treatment loop
  • Community management live, 24h response on both platforms
  • First blog published; on-page audit recommendations delivered
  • Baseline data collection begins, no targets yet, pure measurement
Month 2
Calibration · Rhythm established

Calibration

  • All content pillars in full rotation across both pages
  • Science series Vol. 2: TriPollar RF mechanism deep dive
  • Demand-gen wave one: first landing pages and emails in production
  • Campaign management live with targeting set
  • Mid-point check-in: qualitative read on what is resonating
Month 3
Optimisation · First data pattern

Optimisation

  • Baseline report delivered: first real engagement rate, reach, and adoption data
  • KPI targets set from the actual baseline, not assumptions
  • Content mix adjusted, pillar weights shift on performance
  • Demand-gen wave one results read; second wave briefed
  • Blog library growing; SEO recommendations move to implementation
Months 4–5
Amplification · Full editorial strength

Amplification

  • Science series now a recognized format the audience expects
  • Demand-gen wave two ships; highest-performing formats scaled
  • Distributor enablement framing tracked per asset
  • Evergreen science content compounding across search and social
  • Platform-specific creative testing on LinkedIn and Meta
Month 6
Review · System proven

Review & next levers

  • 6-month performance report: all KPIs vs. baseline, content audit, distributor adoption
  • Recommended scope for the back half of the term: which levers to turn up, which to add
  • Demand-gen wave three scoped; blog and SEO roadmap extended
  • Expansion options priced: TikTok, the Pollogen corporate page, new regions
