01 — What we discussed

Three meetings, one clear picture.

  • /01

    A company at an inflection point in its marketing

    Visit.org has operated for ten years on outbound outreach, conferences, and word of mouth. That approach brought in significant enterprise clients. What comes next requires a more systematic, always-on marketing engine. The infrastructure and the strategy are both being built in parallel, and the opportunity ahead is considerable.

  • /02

    Two products, two markets, two completely different motions

    The enterprise platform is a multi-year SaaS relationship sold through outbound sequences to named accounts and over long sales cycles. Project in a Box is a self-serve e-commerce product with a $65 average order value. It needs paid acquisition, emotional creative, and a Shopify funnel built to convert. Treating these two products with the same approach would mean underperforming on both.

  • /03

    Goodera is real competition, and also a gap to exploit

    The main enterprise competitor has significant headcount, a strong social presence, and aggressive pricing. Critically, Goodera does not actually deliver kits to non-profits. Visit.org is the authentic option, the product with real impact. That story can be told powerfully through creative. It simply has not been told systematically yet.

  • /04

    Enterprise ABM is a natural fit for Visit.org's go-to-market motion

    ABM is well-suited to Visit.org's enterprise business: a defined universe of target accounts, long sales cycles, and decision-makers who respond to personalized, relevant outreach rather than broadcast advertising. The named-account approach, combined with LinkedIn outreach and sequenced email, maps directly onto how enterprise CSR and HR leaders evaluate vendors.

  • /05

    Pilot budget should be calibrated to generate real signal

    A budget that is too low will not provide a real picture of the business potential. Paid acquisition campaigns need sufficient media spend to move through the learning phase and generate statistically meaningful conversion data. The goal of a pilot is not just to run ads; it is to build a data foundation that makes every decision afterward more confident and more accurate.

  • /06

    The pixel needs training before results can happen

    The first four to six weeks of any paid campaign are about feeding the algorithm, not closing deals. That is not a caveat. It is a structural fact of performance marketing that every honest agency tells you upfront. The sooner the pixel starts learning, the sooner costs come down and conversions become predictable.

SERVICE / 01

Enterprise ABM

The right people. The right message. No spray, no pray.

ABM means choosing who you want to work with, building a short list of target accounts, and creating personalized outreach that reaches decision-makers across multiple channels simultaneously. For Visit.org, the targets are HR leaders, CSR managers, and Social Impact directors at companies with 500 to 10,000 employees. They are reachable. They are motivated. A well-run ABM system gets them into the pipeline.

  • Named account list of up to 100 enterprise targets, researched and scored by ICP fit
  • LinkedIn outreach connecting organically with HR and CSR titles, warming them toward a demo conversation
  • Paid LinkedIn campaigns running alongside outbound for multi-touchpoint exposure to the same accounts
  • Intent data via ZoomInfo or Apollo to surface companies actively researching employee engagement
  • Email sequences personalized to company size and the CSR maturity of each account
  • HubSpot pipeline setup to track every touchpoint from first connection to closed contract

08 — How we get started

A phased path that builds momentum from week one.

Each phase has a clear purpose and clear outputs. Nothing starts until the foundation is right.

01

Foundation and setup (Weeks 1 to 2)

HubSpot setup, Shopify pixel and Conversions API installation, competitive landscape review of Goodera and the broader employee engagement market, ICP definition for both verticals, and creative brief for the first UGC batch. No campaigns running yet. Just the infrastructure that makes the campaigns worth running.

02

Pilot launch: SMB acquisition (Weeks 3 to 8)

First UGC creative batch live. Paid social on Meta and LinkedIn targeting HR managers at companies with 50 to 5,000 employees. LinkedIn outreach beginning to connect with enterprise targets in parallel. Pixel learning. First conversion data coming in. Weekly reports, no surprises.

03

Enterprise ABM layer activated (Months 2 to 3)

Named account list finalized. LinkedIn ABM campaigns running. Email sequences launched against the account list. Intent data identifying companies actively researching employee engagement solutions. First enterprise meetings being booked.

04

Scale decision (Month 4 onward)

By this point there is real data. Cost per kit purchase for SMB. Number of enterprise meetings booked and the channels that drove them. Creative performance by format. The question of where to invest more aggressively answers itself, because the data is in the room with us.
