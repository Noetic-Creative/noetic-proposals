SleePare, 6-Month Media Plan to a sub-$100 Book-Appointment CPA

A full-funnel launch across two new channels, engineered to bring cost-per-appointment from a realistic cold-start down to target over six months. Built on SleePare's own Google Ads conversion data.

The Objective

Drive in-store appointment bookings, profitably

The single conversion that matters is a booked appointment (Calendly scheduled) at one of SleePare's nine US showrooms. The target is a cost-per-appointment at or below $100. These are two brand-new channels with no pixel history, so the plan starts deliberately high and converges to target as the algorithms learn.

Where the numbers come from

Anchored to SleePare's real Google Ads data

There is no Meta or Reddit history to model from, so we anchor the appointment CPA to the warmest possible signal we own: SleePare's Google Search, where users arrive with declared intent. That data sets the floor, cold social channels start above it and converge down.

Read: Google's appointment CPA averages ~$134 with a range of $109 to $180, and that is the warmest traffic SleePare has, with a mature pixel and months of optimization. A cold channel with no pixel cannot open below this. That is precisely why Meta is modeled to start at ~$205 and Reddit higher still, then converge as conversion signal accumulates.
Channel 1 · 75% of budget
Meta Ads

Full-funnel: the appointment engine

~$11,250 / month

Meta carries the conversion goal because it is the only channel here with a strong appointment-optimizing engine. We run three stages. The funnel stage is defined by the audience we target, not by the campaign objective. Both MOFU and BOFU optimize for Book Appointment, but they reach different temperatures of audience.

TOFUAwareness
Objective: Video Views / ThruPlay

Warms a cold audience with new video creative edited in-house specifically for Meta. This stage is not judged on CPA; its purpose is to warm the audience so we can retarget those video viewers further down the funnel.

KPI: cost / ThruPlayKPI: 75% video viewedBuilds: 75% video-viewer audiencesCPM $14 to $16
MOFUProspecting
Objective: Sales → Book Appointment · cold audience

Interest + lookalike targeting against people who don't know SleePare yet. This is the prospecting motor and the main volume driver. If it can't exit learning on appointments early, we optimize for an intermediate event (Calendly page view) for 2 to 3 weeks to feed the pixel, then switch.

CPM $13 to $16CTR 0.9% → 1.35%CPC $1.78 → $0.89Freq 2.5 to 3CVR ~1%CPA $205 → $100
BOFURetargeting
Objective: Sales → Book Appointment · warm audience

Same objective, hot audiences: site visitors (180d) and TOFU video viewers (booking-page drop-off retargeting to be confirmed, pending tracking setup). The lowest CPA in the plan. Budget here starts small (no one to retarget at first) and grows as TOFU/MOFU fill the pools.

Freq 5 to 6CPA $95 → $52Audiences: site 180d · VV75% · booking drop-off (TBC)
Budget shift over time: TOFU starts heavy ($3,375 → $1,688) to fill audiences fast, MOFU stays steady (~$5.6 to 6.2k), and BOFU climbs ($1,688 → $3,937) as warm pools grow, which is what pulls the blended CPA down month over month.
Channel 2 · 25% of budget
Reddit Ads

Mid-funnel: consideration & audience feeder

~$3,750 / month

Reddit's conversion optimizer is weaker than Meta's, and the platform naturally fits the consideration stage more than the conversion stage. That is exactly why we use it here: consideration-style campaigns on Reddit complement Meta's TOFU and BOFU work, giving us a full-funnel, cross-channel strategy. Reddit also feeds warm audiences into Meta's retargeting.

Months 1 to 2 · Traffic

Objective: Traffic → showroom landing pages

Conversation-format ads (native posts, not banners) in high-intent communities. Judged on cost-per-click and landing-page views, not appointment CPA, since there's no pixel signal yet to optimize on.

  • Subreddits: r/Mattress, r/SleepApnea, r/BuyItForLife, r/Sleep
  • Interest + community targeting
  • Feeds Meta's retargeting pixel + Reddit's own pixel
CPM $5.50 to 6 CTR 0.5% → 0.6% CPC $1.20 → $0.92 Freq ~1.5 to 2 KPI: cost / landing-page view

Month 3+ · Conversions

Objective: Conversions → Book Appointment

Once the Reddit pixel has accumulated signal, a portion shifts to optimize for appointments. Reddit's direct CPA stays above Meta's throughout, which is expected given its role as a consideration channel rather than a primary conversion driver.

CPM $4.50 to 6 CTR 0.5% → 0.9% CPC $1.20 → $0.50 Freq ~1.5 to 2 Direct CPA $280 → $135
How we judge Reddit: not on its direct appointment CPA, but on cost of generating warm audience and assisted conversions. The appointment is closed by Meta's BOFU; Reddit and Meta's TOFU make that low BOFU CPA possible.
Targeting

Geo-fenced to active showroom cities

Because the conversion is an in-person appointment, both channels are hard geo-fenced to a 25 to 40 km radius around each active showroom, not entire states. Seattle is excluded until it opens (currently "coming soon").

Before we spend a dollar

Pre-launch requirements

The model assumes clean conversion tracking from day one. Neither pixel exists yet, so this is the gating item for the timeline above.

Meta Pixel + CAPI

Install on site, fire Book Appointment on Calendly schedule confirmation. Server-side CAPI for accuracy.

Reddit Pixel

Install on site, map the same appointment event so Reddit can optimize from Month 3.

Creative

New in-house video edits for Meta TOFU, comparison/offer statics for MOFU/BOFU, native conversation posts for Reddit.
