LinkedIn · Key Findings

Four decisions the data made for us.

✓ Winner

Document Ads outperformed everything

Document ads drove the majority of form opens and all qualified lead activity. "See Where Insurance AI Works" hit 11% engagement rate. Static creatives drove zero leads.

✓ Winner

First-party targeting wins on quality

First-party audience (1PT-US) consistently outperformed ABM company list targeting on both engagement rate and lead quality signals. Algorithm found relevant senior personas more efficiently through behavioral signals.

✗ Cut

Website conversion path produced nothing

Campaigns optimized for website conversions generated near-zero leads and were deprioritized mid-flight. Root cause: no dedicated campaign landing pages. Native lead forms are the only viable path today.

→ Insight

Provocative angles unlock engagement

"Most insurance AI projects focus on the wrong problems" and "Why Insurance AI Pilots Fail" showed 4–6% engagement vs. generic messaging. Challenge-based framing cuts through with C-suite and innovation leads.

Google Ads · Key Findings

Three decisions, clear signal.

→ Keep (Lean)

Branded terms: low cost, high value

NC_BrandedTerms_Search is live and eligible. 2,539 impressions · 431 clicks · 16.98% CTR · CPC ~$4.40. No one should lose branded searches to competitors. This runs.

✗ Cut now

Competitor terms: spend with no return

3,844 impressions, 86 clicks, zero conversions. CPC ~$14. Paused. No qualified leads materialized and the landing destination wasn't optimized to capture competitor-aware traffic.

✗ Cut now

Category keywords: expensive, unoptimized

Insurance category KWs drove 693 clicks at ~$7 avg CPC. Tracking misfiring ($0 CPA recorded). Without dedicated landing pages, budget is wasted. Category stays off until pages are built.

Decision: Google budget collapses to branded-only. ~$500/mo. Brand protection, not lead gen. No conversion optimization until campaign landing pages exist.

Month 1 · Success Metrics

How we'll know if
it's working.

🎯Primary

MQL Volume

Target: 8–12 MQLs in Month 1. ICP-matched lead (US insurance carrier · 500+ employees · CIO/COO/VP Innovation) who submits via form. Directional target toward 30 meetings in 90 days.

💰Primary

Cost Per MQL

Target: <$300 CPL (TOF). At $6k budget and 12 target MQLs, implied CPL ceiling is $500. Expect 20–40 TOF leads with 30–40% MQL pass rate via form filtering. Review at Day 14.

📄Secondary

Document Ad KPIs

Form open rate: >4%. CTR: >0.35%. Engagement rate: >3.5%. CPM: $35–55. If CPM exceeds $80, audience is too narrow — expand slightly.

NOT A SUCCESS METRIC

Impressions, reach, or total clicks. We do not optimize for volume. We optimize for ICP-matched contacts only.

The Extra Mile

Three things we're doing
that most agencies won't.

🧠

Challenger messaging framework

Every document ad leads with a tension — a belief the ICP holds that Notch can reframe. We'll build a messaging bank of 6–8 tension statements for Month 2 iteration, so creative decisions are driven by strategic positioning, not guesswork.

🔁

Sales-marketing feedback loop

Every MQL goes through a structured sales quality review. Shared Slack channel where sales flags lead quality within 48h. Media decisions in Week 3 are driven by that feedback, not just platform metrics. This is how CPL improves over time.

📋

Landing page brief on Day 1

The biggest unlock for Month 2 is a campaign landing page for category keywords. We deliver a wireframe-level spec for the insurance vertical page within the first week. This unblocks Google category KWs and improves conversion from all paid traffic.
