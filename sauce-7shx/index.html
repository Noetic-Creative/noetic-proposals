01 — The setup

Sauce isn't fighting a POS war.
It's fighting for ownership of the order.

The strategic frame.

Sauce occupies an unclaimed territory: financial justice for restaurant owners. Not commission-free as a feature. Ownership as a worldview. A coalition of operators who refuse to keep paying 30% to platforms in San Francisco for the customers they earned.

The restaurant industry digitized, then it got trapped. Off-premise traffic for full-service restaurants grew from 19% in 2019 to 30% in 2024, and 95% of operators now use at least one online ordering platform. Owners said yes to the platforms because they had to. The platforms controlled the demand.

Sauce is the only platform in this category where "Every order, 100% yours" maps 1:1 to the actual business model. Owner.com still charges a 5% customer fee. Popmenu still routes delivery through DoorDash. Lunchbox builds for enterprise chains, not the mom-and-pop and small-chain operators Sauce serves.

That distinction is the engagement. Every dollar of paid search spend, every UGC concept, every landing page edit ladders back to one truth: this is the only platform where you keep all of it. The order, the customer, the data, the margin.

"30% wasn't convenience. It was extortion. Nobody is saying the quiet part."
02 — What we heard today

Six things from the audit and the conversation that shape this proposal.

  • /01

    Search alone won't scale in 2026.

    91.7% of current paid spend sits in Search. The brand campaign is budget-capped at less than 10% impression share. Non-brand competes against DoorDash on rising CPCs while AI Overviews compress the SERP. The ceiling is real, and the answer is diversification, not more search budget.

  • /02

    The campaigns are bidding to the wrong action.

    57 SQLs and 33 Customers are tracked in HubSpot over the last 12 months. The campaigns optimize to mid-funnel HubSpot Opportunity. Google's machine learning is being trained on the wrong signal. Move bidding to SQLs and Customers. Move to value-based bidding with weighted conversions. Train the model on who buys, not who fills forms.

  • /03

    Performance Max and Demand Gen are sleeping.

    Less than 5% of spend each. PMax unlocks YouTube, Display, Gmail, Discover at lower CPCs than search keywords, but only if you optimize it for lower-funnel actions and feed it real audience signals. Today it runs on a single general asset group with no first-party data import. Tim estimates upper-funnel could eventually produce up to half of final sales volume.

  • /04

    The HubSpot list is dormant.

    One GDN retargeting conversion year-to-date. Sauce already has a qualified-leads list ready to activate. Importing those audiences for retargeting, exclusion, and PMax audience signals is a Day-30 lift, not a Day-90 lift. It compounds from there.

  • /05

    The message lands. The page doesn't catch it.

    "Delivery fees don't have to be 30%" earns the click. The current landing page asks for a 10/10 commitment with a 2/10 trust foundation. The conversion dies in that gap. The size of the conversion ask must equal the size of the trust built before it. Creative, copy, and CRO close the gap.

  • /06

    The ownership story is the whole story.

    "Every order, 100% yours" is the only claim no competitor can structurally make. Every channel, every ad group, every creative concept, every landing page should ladder back to that one truth. The territory is already differentiated. The job is to put it everywhere a restaurant owner can find it.

04 — IN BOTH TIERS

Paid Search overhaul

Train the model on who buys, not who fills forms.

Today's account is built around mid-funnel signals and one channel. The next twelve months rebuild it around lower-funnel signals and four. Every change below maps to something Tim flagged in the audit and stress-tested with Kfir on the call.

  • Conversion bidding moved to SQL + Customer, with weighted value-based bidding
  • Tightly themed ad groups, with ads and landing-page direction matched to query intent
  • Brand and non-brand fully separated, with non-brand excluding brand terms
  • Negative keyword shared lists deployed across the account
  • Performance Max with multiple asset groups + HubSpot first-party signals
  • Demand Gen for retargeting visitors and unconverted leads
  • Display retargeting scaled from the HubSpot qualified-leads list
  • Custom audience targeting for competitor interest, instead of bidding on competitor terms
  • Server-side GTM and Conversion API coordination with your data partner
  • Geographic targeting that balances metro focus with cost-effective adjacencies
/ 01

Train on real customers

Optimize to SQL and Customer. Stop training Google on form-fills that don't translate to revenue. Move toward value-based bidding once volume permits.

/ 02

Build upper funnel

Activate PMax and Demand Gen with lower-funnel goals and rich asset groups. Search CPC caps scale fast. PMax doesn't.

/ 03

Customize the journey

Themed ad groups feed matching ads, which feed matching landing pages. Eliminate the gap where "food delivery software" sits inside a "Commission Free Delivery" ad group.

/ 04

Activate first-party data

Import HubSpot lead and customer lists. Use SQL and Customer lists as PMax audience signals. Exclude existing customers from acquisition campaigns.

06 — The 90-day plan

First 30 days, foundation. Next 60, compounding optimization.

The first quarter is foundational. Onboarding, ICP and messaging work, conversion infrastructure, the audit playbook executed end-to-end. Soft successes are realistic in the first three months. Meaningful ROI and volume growth compound from there.

Days 0 – 30 · Foundation

Onboard, audit, instrument.

Kick off with the Sauce team. Lock the messaging architecture and ICP definition with Caro and Alex. Coordinate with Kfir on server-side GTM, Conversion API, and HubSpot integration. Import qualified-leads list. Pause non-performing campaigns. Stand up the dashboard layer.

  • Conversion tracking and server-side GTM coordinated
  • HubSpot lists imported, customer exclusion lists deployed
  • Account audit findings translated into a prioritized backlog
02
Days 30 – 60 · Build

Restructure search. Activate upper funnel.

Rebuild ad groups around tight intent themes. Move bidding to SQL and Customer. Separate brand and non-brand. Deploy negative keyword shared lists. Stand up Performance Max with multiple asset groups and first-party signals. Begin Demand Gen for retargeting unconverted leads. (Tier 2: first UGC batch live and in market.)

  • Search campaigns restructured and re-bid
  • PMax with multiple asset groups and HubSpot signals live
  • GDN retargeting from qualified-leads list active
  • Tier 2 only: first three UGC concepts in paid rotation
03
Days 60 – 90 · Optimize

Read signal. Cut, double, iterate.

First clean read on lower-funnel signal quality. Bid adjustments based on SQL and Customer attribution. Negative keyword expansion. Asset group performance triage in PMax. Demand Gen audience expansion. Landing page CRO recommendations delivered. (Tier 2: second UGC batch with hypothesis-driven variants.)

  • Bid adjustments tied to lower-funnel signal quality
  • Asset group and creative iteration in PMax and Demand Gen
  • Landing page CRO direction delivered to your web team
  • Quarterly performance review with the Sauce leadership team
04
Months 4 – 12 · Compound

Scale what's working. Defend what's not.

Predictable cadence. Monthly performance reviews. Bi-weekly working sessions with Kfir. Quarterly strategic reviews with Sauce leadership. Volume scaling on proven channels, hypothesis-driven testing on adjacent ones. Year-over-year customer-volume growth at stable or improving CAC is the success metric.
