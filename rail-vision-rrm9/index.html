Account-Based Marketing

Your list is the strategy. We build the machine that works it.

When your total addressable market is a named list, ABM isn't a niche tactic — it's the only tactic that makes sense. The work is identifying the right people inside each account, mapping the buying committee, and running campaigns that speak to each persona's specific concerns. Not lead generation. Meaningful engagement.

  • ICP expansion from client's Tier 1 account list to Tier 2 and Tier 3
  • Buying committee mapping per account: champion, finance, operations, executive leadership
  • Multi-persona LinkedIn ad sequences, each speaking to a distinct pain point
  • Intent signal monitoring — identifying when target accounts engage with content or visit key pages
  • Weekly sales and marketing handoff: which accounts to prioritize, what signals fired

Webinar Engine

Long sales cycles need more touchpoints, not louder ones.

When deals take 18 months to close, the goal isn't to generate a lead once. It's to stay meaningfully in front of the right people over a long period. Webinars are the most efficient format for that: substantive enough to attract serious buyers, structured enough to repeat, and measurable at every step. Noetic handles the entire operation — you bring the topic and the speaker.

  • Full platform management: setup, registration pages, attendee tracking, run-of-show
  • Pre-event email campaign to target account list and known contacts
  • In-event follow-up sequence triggered by attendance and engagement level
  • Post-event nurture flow for non-attendees (recording, follow-on content)
  • Reporting: registrations, attendance rate, engagement score, HubSpot attribution

03 — HubSpot lifecycle

The system is live. The architecture isn't built yet.

HubSpot is most valuable when it knows what to do with a contact at every stage of the funnel. Right now, Rail Vision has the platform. What it doesn't yet have is the logic layer: segmentation, scoring, flows, and the handoff protocol that tells the BDR exactly when to reach out and why.

SERVICE / 03

HubSpot Lifecycle

Infrastructure that makes your BDR's outreach smarter every month.

For a named-account business, HubSpot isn't a CRM for managing volume. It's an intelligence layer. Done right, it tells you which accounts are heating up, which contacts have been touched and how many times, and which leads are ready for a rep to call.

  • Contact and company segmentation by account tier and ICP fit
  • Lead scoring model calibrated to Rail Vision's buying signals
  • Automated nurture sequences triggered by content engagement
  • Pipeline stage definitions and sales handoff protocols
  • Reporting dashboard: MQL velocity, account engagement score, sequence performance
Step 01 — Foundation

Segment and score

Map your Apollo contacts into HubSpot with Tier 1, 2, and 3 account classifications. Build a lead scoring model that weights intent signals — email opens, webinar attendance, page visits, content downloads — so you always know which accounts to prioritize.

Step 02 — Nurture

Build the flows

Three to five automated sequences covering the core journey stages: new contact welcome, cold account re-engagement, post-webinar follow-up, and pre-demo warm-up. Each flow is persona-aware — what a rail operations director receives is different from what a procurement director receives.

Step 03 — Handoff

Make the BDR's job easier

When a contact hits a certain engagement score or completes a specific sequence, the BDR gets a task in HubSpot with context: which touchpoints fired, what content they engaged with, which account tier they belong to. No more cold outreach into warm accounts.

Ongoing

Optimize and report

Monthly reporting on sequence open rates, click rates, MQL volume, and pipeline attribution. What's working gets scaled. What isn't gets revised. HubSpot only gets smarter the longer it's running with clean data.
