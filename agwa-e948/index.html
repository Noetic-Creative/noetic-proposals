The setup

Your cabinet grows lettuce
autonomously. Your HubSpot
doesn't grow anything — yet.

Here's the thing that struck us about Agwa during our conversation: your entire product is a story about systems that run themselves. The cabinet handles the water, the nutrients, the pH. The crew on the ship doesn't have to think about it. They just open the door and pick lettuce.

Right now, your HubSpot is the opposite of that. It's a closed cabinet with the lights off. You have Deals. You have Contacts. You have Accounts. And nothing — no leads, no signal, no pipeline — flows through it autonomously. Eli is doing his thing. The BDR firm is doing their thing. Your creative person is making beautiful work that nobody can systematically measure. The CEO is pushing weekly to move faster.

The product Agwa sells should be the model for the marketing system Agwa runs. Both should grow things on a schedule, without anyone watching.

You asked for three tiers because you want a real choice — not just three prices for the same thing. So that's what we built. Each tier is a meaningfully different version of the system. Each one solves for a different version of "we need this to work."

The math behind the tiers is simple: Tier 1 turns the lights on. Tier 2 makes the system useful. Tier 3 makes it run by itself. Which one is right for you depends on a few things we'll walk through. First, let's recap what we heard.

What you need

Four things a real HubSpot
system gives you.

Speak to every ICP without rebuilding
You have at least three Maritime personas — captain, ops, compliance officer — and Offshore, Senior Care, and Government inspectors are all coming. One generic "maritime audience" won't carry you. A real segmentation architecture lets you talk to a ship captain about uptime, and to a compliance officer about Dept of Agriculture standards, from the same database — without rebuilding when the next ICP arrives.
Unify outbound, inbound, and BDR in one pipeline
Excel BDR is running outbound. Eli closes from his phone. Website inbound trickles in. Right now, none of these flow into a single attributed view. A unified system means a lead the BDR books on Monday and a website form Wednesday land in the same place — so Eli never works the same lead twice, and you can see which channel actually drives revenue.
Nurture leads without anyone touching it
You have one marketer who's "all about Figma, not HubSpot." Manually nurturing every lead isn't realistic, and adding headcount before fundraising isn't on the table. Lifecycle automation runs without anyone touching it. Your creative person designs the message once — welcome flow, nurture sequence, re-engagement — and the system delivers it forever.
Reporting your CEO and investors will trust
Pre-fundraising, you'll be asked: what's the cost per qualified lead? Which channels are working? What's the marketing-influenced pipeline? These need clean answers. Real dashboards — pipeline contribution by source, lifecycle conversion rates, channel ROI — are non-negotiable for the next CEO weekly and investor conversation.

Multi-Touch Nurture Flows

Lead Scoring System
18 Multi-attribute scoring with score-based routing
  • Behavioral signals: visited pricing page (+10), downloaded spec sheet (+15), attended demo (+25), opened 3+ emails (+5), replied (+30)
  • Demographic signals: matches Maritime ICP (+10), decision-maker title (+15), Tier-1 named account (+20)
  • Negative signals: unsubscribed (−50), hard-bounced (−20), competitor email (−50)
  • Routing: 50+ = MQL alert, 75+ = SQL alert to Eli, 100+ = priority alert to all three of you
Account-Level Intelligence

Plus an offline conversion import workflow — Eli closes a deal from a phone call, it flows back into HubSpot for proper attribution. Critical given Excel BDR is making outbound calls.

How to choose

A framework, not a recommendation.

Each tier solves a different problem. Here's how to know which problem is yours.
Pick Tier 1 if
You need to start, not finish.
  • →Budget is the binding constraint and you can revisit fuller scope in 60 days.
  • →You want to validate HubSpot is the right tool before deeper investment.
  • →An in-house HubSpot owner is starting soon and they'll build the rest.
Trade-off You'll see leads but you won't yet have the system to act on them at scale.
Pick Tier 2 if
You want a working system, not a project.
  • →You need to talk to a Maritime captain differently from a Senior Care administrator.
  • →Your creative person needs templates that actually let them send things.
  • →You want it operational and self-sufficient before you bring HubSpot in-house.
Trade-off Well-segmented one-offs, not yet automated nurtures running in the background.
Pick Tier 3 if
You want the engine running before Gen 2 lands.
  • →The CEO is pushing on velocity and you want everything live now, not in stages.
  • →Eli's outbound calls need to flow back into HubSpot for proper attribution.
  • →Pre-funding visibility — you want a real revenue engine in due-diligence decks.
Trade-off Mid-market scope at one-off pricing — biggest ROI long-term, biggest investment upfront.

If two of these sound right, you're probably in that tier. If you're between two, the lower one is usually the safer bet — we can always extend scope mid-engagement, but we can't unbuild what's already shipped. Happy to talk through any of this on a call.
