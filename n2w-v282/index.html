01 — What we discussed

A 65-minute conversation that covered a lot of real ground.

On May 18, Iris Abramovich and Tamir Alush connected for the first time. Here is what was covered.

  • /01

    N2W's product and market position

    N2W is a cloud-native backup and disaster recovery platform for AWS and Azure environments. The product recovers critical systems in under 60 seconds, and the company now serves over 1,000 enterprise customers. Competitors are Veeam, Rubrik, and Commvault. Clients include Mercedes, Coca-Cola, Wix, and Check Point. The primary growth target is the US enterprise market, with EMEA (UK, Germany) and APAC as secondary lanes.

  • /02

    A clear ICP across five verticals

    Technology and software companies, financial services, healthcare, public sector and government, and engineering and industrial. Within those, the buying personas are: Head of IT and IT Directors as champions, Cloud Ops and DevOps as technical influencers, and Finance or Operations leadership as budget and risk approvers. Healthcare in the US was called out as the top priority vertical to penetrate.

  • /03

    ABM discussed as the missing piece

    N2W has run ABM in an ad hoc way: giving a list of 20 companies to an agency, getting some names back, running some paid ads. Iris described wanting something more structured with proper persona mapping, channel selection, and a real test-and-iterate cadence. Tamir walked through the 3-stakeholder model (champion, budget owner, operational risk holder) and Iris responded directly: "exactly like that."

  • /04

    Video and UGC: the biggest gap called out

    Tamir's own post-meeting notes flagged this as the top missing capability: UGC video and video coverage at conferences. Iris was shown examples of human-actor UGC content (DataRails-style short videos) and responded directly: "I love this more." N2W has a strong in-house design team but no systematic creator video program and no structured on-site conference content capture. This was the most clearly articulated gap of the conversation.

  • /05

    Webinars operating below their potential

    N2W runs approximately one webinar per month. Product update webinars draw around 300 registrants at roughly 50% attendance rates. Non-product webinars are still building traction. Iris wants to use webinars as a more deliberate lead generation and pipeline acceleration lever, and is planning a mix of customer-led, partner-led, product-focused, and industry hot-topic formats.

  • /06

    Organic social presence is thin and recognized as such

    LinkedIn is N2W's main organic channel. The account is active, with conference announcements and product news, but follower count and engagement are not where Iris wants them. She described wanting a partner who could build a real content strategy and growth plan for the account, not just manage posts. Tamir shared how Noetic builds LinkedIn presence for companies like Lumenis, including trade show content capture and regular editorial cadence.

02 — The strategic frame

Three buyers.
One deal. Time for committee-level precision.

N2W's product value is clearest in moments of crisis: an AWS East-1 thermal event takes down Coinbase and CME Group for hours. A ransomware hit freezes a hospital's records. An Azure outage grounds a logistics network. The buyers who sign N2W contracts have three different jobs in the same organization, and each needs a different reason to care. Broadcast campaigns do not reach them. A 3-persona ABM motion does.

Persona 01 — The Champion

Head of IT / Cloud Ops

The daily user of N2W's platform. Owns the backup policy, the RTO/RPO targets, the compliance audit. Feels the pain directly when an AWS region goes down. This is the person who advocates internally for the solution.

Zero-downtime recovery in under 60 seconds, across AWS and Azure, without scripts or agents.
Persona 02 — The Budget Owner

CFO / Finance Lead

Signs the contract. Cares about cost reduction, not features. N2W offers up to 92% savings on backup storage costs and a measurable ROI argument: what does two hours of cloud downtime actually cost the business?

Save up to 92% on backup storage. Downtime costs more than the subscription.
Persona 03 — The Risk Owner

COO / Head of Operations

Accountable for business continuity. A regional AWS outage is a board-level conversation. Ransomware is a headline. N2W is the insurance policy that lets operations leaders sleep. The message is risk, not technology.

When the cloud goes down, operations recover in seconds. Not hours. Not days.
SERVICE / 01
Account-Based Marketing
Precision targeting for enterprise IT committees.

ABM is not a channel. It is a framework. Noetic builds a named-account program that runs coordinated messaging to all three buyer personas inside every target company, using paid, email, and content in parallel.

  • Persona mapping across IT, Finance, and Operations roles within each account
  • Ad creative and landing pages tailored per persona and per vertical (Healthcare, FinServ, TechSW)
  • LinkedIn and display retargeting sequenced by funnel stage
  • Monthly performance reporting with account-level engagement scoring
