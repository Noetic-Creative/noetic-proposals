01 — What we discussed

Six things from April 27 worth putting on record.

  • /01

    The market is 100 to 200 named accounts.

    Doron was direct: this is not a volume-marketing problem. The total addressable universe of rail operators and relevant freight and passenger companies in the US and Europe fits on one list. You do not need a great targeting algorithm. You need infrastructure that makes those specific 200 accounts feel specifically reached.

  • /02

    Outbound is already moving. The warm layer is not built yet.

    An internal BDR is hired and Apollo is in use. The cold outreach motion exists. What is missing is the layer that makes the warm calls land: brand familiarity, consistent content presence, and visible proof that Rail Vision is a category leader before the first conversation happens.

  • /03

    HubSpot is installed but not yet activated.

    The CRM is in place. Lifecycle stages, nurture flows, and automated sequences are still being built. For a business with multi-year sales cycles and a small account universe, the gap between "HubSpot exists" and "HubSpot is working for us" is the difference between data and momentum.

  • /04

    Railway News is a distribution channel, not a demand creation engine.

    Yeela was clear: Railway News reaches a wide subscriber base across the rail industry and puts content in front of the right people. It is a reach tool. It does not build the strategy, run the campaigns, or produce the pipeline infrastructure. The two are not in competition. They are solving different problems.

  • /05

    ABM, webinars, and LinkedIn were the three channels that resonated.

    We walked through several options. These three kept getting pulled back into the conversation. Specifically, the Sapiens webinar data (50% higher close probability for leads with webinar touchpoints across 3-year sales cycles) landed as the most direct parallel to Rail Vision's reality.

  • /06

    The right first step is a discovery sprint, not a retainer pitch.

    Tamir suggested, and both Doron and Yeela agreed, that the right sequence is: short discovery work first, then a tailored proposal based on what we learn. A generic retainer quote does not serve anyone here. The market is too specific for that.

It is exactly the kind of infrastructure we build.

SERVICE / 03

HubSpot
Lifecycle

The plumbing is there. The flows are not.

Having HubSpot is not the same as HubSpot working for you. For Rail Vision, with a small account universe and long sales cycles, the difference between a CRM that tracks activity and one that actively moves contacts through a pipeline is the difference between a spreadsheet and a revenue engine.

  • Lifecycle stage definition and contact segmentation by account tier
  • Nurture sequence design across top, mid, and bottom of funnel
  • Webinar and event integration: automatic enrollment and follow-up
  • BDR activity logging, deal stage triggers, and handoff automations
  • Reporting dashboards: pipeline velocity, sequence performance, contact engagement
/ 01

Lifecycle stages that match how rail deals actually move

Generic HubSpot templates do not work for enterprise B2B with 3-year cycles. We define stages that reflect your actual sales motion, from first awareness through multiple buying committee touchpoints to closed-won.

/ 02

Sequences that run while your BDR focuses on relationships

The 180 days between a promising conversation and the next RFP should not go dark. Automated nurture sequences keep Rail Vision visible and useful throughout the full cycle.

/ 03

ABM and webinar data flowing into the same place

Campaign engagement, webinar attendance, email opens, website visits, all feeding into contact and account records so your team always knows where each account stands and what they have seen.

/ 04

A dashboard that tells you which accounts are warming up

Not just activity counts. Pipeline-velocity reporting that surfaces which accounts are engaging more frequently, which sequences are driving replies, and where the BDR's time is best spent this week.

A note on the current state: you mentioned HubSpot is installed but still being fine-tuned. That is actually the ideal starting point. Building the lifecycle architecture at this stage, before habits form, is significantly easier than retrofitting it later. A discovery sprint would include a full HubSpot audit as a foundational deliverable.
