Two meetings. A clear picture of where to begin.

From the April 2 call with Michal to the April 16 session with Michal, Edan, and Alex, here is what we walked away understanding.

  • /01

    Nayax wants partners, not vendors

    Michal was direct about this from the first conversation: the team is substantial and capable, but the goal is to remove execution friction and bring in partners who think like owners. The scope on the table is not a backfill. It is a relationship built around trusted external execution.

  • /02

    UGC is the immediate priority. Ten units, $1,500 each.

    Edan and Alex confirmed a 10-unit UGC package at $1,500 per unit as the starting engagement. Alex is preparing the brief and messaging document. The first batch is targeting US operator acquisition, with Mexico as the next market as volume scales.

  • /03

    Retargeting is offline. Meta blocked the pixel.

    Meta blocked Nayax's pixel, which means retargeting to site visitors and warm audiences is currently not running. With a US media budget of $30 to 40K, this is a meaningful gap. Rebuilding the retargeting motion requires new creative assets alongside resolving the pixel issue through a clean account and domain setup.

  • /04

    LATAM is a real strategic expansion, not a side market

    Michal flagged Brazil and LATAM explicitly as markets where Nayax needs localized user acquisition and messaging support. Mexico is active but underfunded at $2K media spend. Nayax has made acquisitions in Brazil specifically to anchor LATAM infrastructure, and the expectation is that marketing investment follows.

  • /05

    Payter flagged as a competitor worth targeting in EV charging

    Tamir raised Payter's EV charging vertical as a displacement opportunity. The play is straightforward: content and paid campaigns that intercept operators researching payment solutions for EV stations, positioned against Payter's specific limitations. Michal was receptive to this framing.

  • /06

    The budget signals this is a long-term engagement

    Total marketing budget excluding headcount is $5M. Media budget is $1.5M annually. This is not a trial. The investment level tells us Nayax is building a serious, multi-channel demand generation operation, and the right partner earns access to a substantial recurring scope across channels and markets.

ABM for Operator Segments

Target the 300 accounts that matter. Skip the rest.

Nayax's operator base is highly definable. EV charging network operators. Multi-unit vending route operators. Laundromat chains. Micro-market groups. These are named companies with named contacts and identifiable buying triggers. That makes this a strong ABM play.

  • Target account lists built by vertical: EV operators, vending chains, laundromat groups
  • LinkedIn ABM via Influ2 targeting specific titles across operator buying committees
  • Competitor displacement campaign targeting Payter accounts in EV charging
  • Personalized landing pages per vertical with segment-specific messaging
  • HubSpot lead scoring and ABM attribution for clean pipeline reporting to Edan
