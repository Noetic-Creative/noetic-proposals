01 — What we discussed

The shape of the brand, the shape of the market, and the assets we have to work with.

  • /01

    Distribution is the channel. Distributors are the audience.

    Pollogen sells through distributors, not direct to clinics or estheticians. That changes the role of marketing. Content cannot just create visibility. It has to help distributors sell with more confidence and give them a stronger reason to order more. Distributors are the first audience we win, and the rest of the system bends around that fact.

  • /02

    Two brands, distinct roles. Geneo is the star, Pollogen is the corporate frame.

    Around 80 to 90 percent of current investment sits behind Geneo. It earned that focus. Pollogen, the master brand, currently mirrors Geneo on Instagram and reads as a corporate authority surface, with around 4,000 followers to Geneo's 1,000. The architecture we agreed on is closer to Luminous corporate plus Luminous Aesthetic than a clean B2B versus B2C split. Pollogen carries the science and portfolio breadth. Geneo carries the consumer pull. Both balance the three audiences.

  • /03

    The market is more layered than two regions.

    Europe and China was a useful starting frame. The reality is wider. Western and Eastern Europe behave differently. The Philippines, India, and other regions each carry their own distributor relationships, regulatory shapes, and content expectations. China specifically: Tripolar was sold to a different firm and is no longer Pollogen's to grow there, but Geneo lives in China with a distinct motion. A multi-region brand needs a multi-region strategy. Built one region at a time.

  • /04

    The science is the under-told story.

    Pollogen has the technology and clinical credibility many brands would invest heavily to claim. Today the communication leans toward beauty and lifestyle, while the science backbone stays quiet. The white space is the connection between real clinical depth and consumer-legible storytelling. Practical performance, backed by real science, accessible without being entry-level, professional without being out of reach. Caro's working creative platform, "your beauty unlocked by biology," sits inside this territory.

  • /05

    The Glo2Facial situation is a content engine waiting to be repurposed.

    Glo2Facial is essentially Geneo in the United States, run today by an EMEA-managed team with a meaningful budget, a marketing org of around ten, and high-profile collaborations. Pollogen produces the materials. Yael is working with their VP of marketing, Kirsten, to bring the brand back under direct Geneo control. Once that lands, Geneo content from the US program (KOL collaborations included) can be repurposed across Pollogen's global channels. That is real leverage, not yet activated.

  • /06

    An in-house esthetician is an asset most brands do not have.

    Pollogen has a practicing esthetician at the office in Israel, available for treatments, photo, and video on demand. That removes a production bottleneck most brands spend months solving. The trade-off is geographic concentration, which means we still need a KOL pipeline through distributors in priority regions. But the in-house production loop is real, and we should design content systems that make full use of it.

  • /07

    The path is depth before tactics. Yael was clear on that.

    We presented a working frame: three audiences, four content pillars, a creative platform, a distribution mix across Instagram, TikTok, and LinkedIn. The response was the right one. The tactical content shape is familiar from the Luminous work. What is not yet familiar is the brand architecture, the regional motions, the distributor reality, and the budget shape. A real briefing session comes first. Then the proposal sharpens. Then the work begins.

02 — How we're framing the work

Activate what's already there.

Pollogen does not need a new brand. It needs an editorial system that releases the brand it already has. The work is sequencing. Brief depth, then the regional map, then the production engine, then paid as the distribution layer.

LANE A · ASSETS

What's already there.

  • Real clinical science. Tripolar, DMA, oxypod systems, the published proof points behind them.
  • Geneo brand pull. The flagship is working. Eighty percent of investment is behind it for a reason.
  • An in-house esthetician. Production capacity most agencies negotiate for months.
  • An existing distributor network. Multiple regions, real shelf presence, real demand signal.
  • Glo2Facial's US content engine. Repurposable once the brand realignment lands.
  • The Luminous continuity. Vanessa is the same AM. Four years of medical-aesthetic editorial muscle, ready.

The brand is not starting from zero. Treat it that way.

LANE B · THE WORK

What needs to happen next.

  • A real brand briefing. Architecture, portfolio, Maximus launch, regional realities, budget shape.
  • A region-by-region map. Europe (East and West), China, Philippines, India, others. Each its own motion.
  • A distributor enablement library. Content distributors can use, not just content distributors see.
  • A KOL pipeline through distributors. Direct clinic relationships are not the lever. Distributor-introduced KOLs are.
  • An editorial system across two pages. Pollogen as authority register. Geneo as consumer pull. One voice, two surfaces.
  • Paid as a layer, not an engine. Tight budget sharpens the targeting, not the ambition.

Earn the depth before earning the ship date.

SERVICE / 03

UGC, KOLs & Creative Production

Treatment moments at scale, without losing the science.

UGC is not volume. It is the right voice in the right register. For a brand selling through distributors into multiple regions, that means content distributors can use with their own KOL networks, and content that arrives in feeds feeling authentic, not staged.

Pollogen has a real production advantage in the in-house esthetician. We design the system around it, then layer in distributor-introduced KOLs in priority regions to round out diversity and credibility. Every asset is built with second-life in mind: distributor decks, conference reels, paid creative, organic posts.

  • In-house production loops that fully use the on-site esthetician
  • KOL acquisition through distributor partnerships, not direct outreach
  • B2C UGC formats: real-routine, in-bathroom, friend-posted-it
  • B2B and clinic UGC: practitioner POV, patient journey, treatment room
  • Asset packaging for distributor reuse, with brand guardrails baked in

SERVICE / 04

Paid Media: Boosting & Distribution

Tight budgets sharpen the work, they do not shrink it.

When the budget is small and the geography is global, paid is a distribution layer, not a demand-gen engine. The work is matching the right post to the right moment in the right region, not running campaigns in parallel.

For Pollogen, that means a boosting layer on top of the editorial system. Europe in phase one, China and other priority regions in phase two, calibrated against the budget Yael and Tamir define together. Light B2B targeting on LinkedIn for distributor and decision-maker visibility. Meta and TikTok as discovery surfaces, not conversion endpoints.

  • Boosting integrated with the editorial calendar, not run beside it
  • Geographic activation by region, sequenced not simultaneous
  • Light B2B targeting on LinkedIn for distributor and clinic visibility
  • Meta and TikTok as discovery surfaces sized to the available budget
  • Quarterly redirection based on what is earning attention organically
